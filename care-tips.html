Cat Care Tips

Everything you need to know to keep your feline friend healthy and happy

🍽️ Nutrition & Feeding

Quality Diet

  • Feed high-quality cat food formulated for your cat's age (kitten, adult, or senior)
  • Look for foods with named meat as the first ingredient
  • Avoid foods with artificial preservatives and colors
  • Mix wet and dry food for hydration and dental health

Feeding Schedule

  • Kittens (under 1 year): Feed 3-4 times per day
  • Adult cats: Feed once or twice daily
  • Senior cats (over 10 years): Feed smaller, more frequent meals
  • Always provide fresh, clean water

Foods to Avoid

  • Chocolate - toxic to cats
  • Onions and garlic - can damage red blood cells
  • Grapes and raisins - can cause kidney failure
  • Xylitol - artificial sweetener found in many products
  • Raw dough and eggs

🧼 Grooming & Hygiene

Regular Grooming

  • Brush long-haired cats daily to prevent matting
  • Brush short-haired cats 2-3 times per week
  • Trim nails every 2-3 weeks
  • Clean ears weekly with a damp cloth
  • Brush teeth 2-3 times per week to prevent dental disease

Litter Box Care

  • Scoop litter daily
  • Replace litter completely 1-2 times per week
  • Keep litter box in a quiet, accessible location
  • Use 1-2 litter boxes per cat (plus one extra)
  • Keep boxes away from food and water

Bathing Tips

  • Most cats groom themselves and don't need baths
  • Bathe only if necessary (dirty coat, health issues)
  • Use lukewarm water (around 100°F / 37°C)
  • Use cat-specific shampoo, never human shampoo
  • Have towels ready and work quickly

💪 Health & Wellness

Veterinary Care

  • Schedule annual vet check-ups for healthy adults
  • Senior cats (over 10 years) should visit twice yearly
  • Keep vaccinations current
  • Spay or neuter your cat to prevent health and behavioral problems
  • Get a microchip and maintain updated identification

Health Monitoring

  • Watch for changes in appetite, weight, or behavior
  • Monitor litter box habits (frequency and consistency)
  • Check for signs of illness: vomiting, diarrhea, lethargy, coughing
  • Feel your cat's body regularly for lumps or abnormalities
  • Keep a record of your cat's weight and health notes

Common Health Issues

  • Obesity: Monitor portion sizes and provide exercise
  • Dental Disease: Regular brushing and dental checkups
  • Kidney Disease: More common in senior cats
  • Diabetes: Can be managed with diet and medication
  • Arthritis: Provide comfortable bedding for senior cats

🎮 Enrichment & Play

Interactive Play

  • Play with your cat for 15-30 minutes daily
  • Use interactive toys like laser pointers (use responsibly) and feather wands
  • Rotate toys to maintain interest
  • Playtime helps with exercise and mental stimulation
  • Best play times are early morning and evening (natural hunting times)

Environmental Enrichment

  • Provide scratching posts to satisfy natural scratching instincts
  • Install cat trees or shelves for climbing and perching
  • Window perches for watching birds and outdoor activity
  • Hide treats around the house for foraging behavior
  • Provide boxes and paper bags for hiding and play

Mental Stimulation

  • Puzzle feeders and treat toys engage their problem-solving skills
  • Train your cat with positive reinforcement clicker training
  • Teach tricks like sit, high-five, and come
  • Provide food dispensers that require interaction
  • Keep their environment varied and interesting

🏠 Home Safety

🌿 Toxic Plants

  • Lilies (very dangerous)
  • Oleander
  • Azaleas and rhododendrons
  • Sago palms
  • Dieffenbachia

Safe alternatives: Spider plants, cat grass, Boston ferns

🔌 Hazards to Avoid

  • Exposed electrical cords
  • Open windows without screens
  • Small objects that can be swallowed
  • Plastic bags and string
  • Cleaning chemicals and medications

Prevention: Store safely and supervise play areas

🏡 Comfortable Spaces

  • Multiple hideaway spots
  • Cozy beds in quiet areas
  • Vertical spaces for escape
  • Dark, enclosed spaces
  • Temperature-controlled areas

Benefit: Reduces stress and anxiety

📋 Quick Health Checklist

Daily:

  • ✓ Fresh water and food
  • ✓ Clean litter box
  • ✓ Playtime and interaction
  • ✓ Check for any changes in behavior

Weekly:

  • ✓ Brush your cat's coat
  • ✓ Check ears and eyes
  • ✓ Trim nails if needed
  • ✓ Deep clean litter box

Monthly:

  • ✓ Check weight and body condition
  • ✓ Inspect teeth and mouth
  • ✓ Review diet adequacy
  • ✓ Check for fleas and parasites

Annually:

  • ✓ Veterinary check-up
  • ✓ Vaccination updates
  • ✓ Blood work (especially for senior cats)
  • ✓ Dental cleaning if needed

⚠️ When to Call the Vet

Seek immediate veterinary care if your cat shows:

  • Difficulty breathing or choking
  • Severe injury or bleeding
  • Inability to urinate or defecate
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Repeated vomiting or diarrhea
  • Extreme lethargy or weakness
  • Sudden behavioral changes
  • Signs of severe pain

Don't wait to call if something seems wrong! Trust your instincts - early intervention can save lives.
