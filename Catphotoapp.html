What Do Cats Love & Hate?

💚 Things Cats Love

  • 🧶 Playing with toys and strings
  • 😴 Comfortable napping spots
  • 🐭 Hunting (real or toy prey)
  • 💆 Gentle petting sessions
  • 🪟 Window perches for bird watching
  • 🎵 Soft music and calm environments

💔 Things Cats Hate

  • 💊 Medication and vet visits
  • ⛈️ Loud noises and thunderstorms
  • 🚿 Unexpected baths
  • 🎆 Fireworks and loud celebrations
  • 🏃 Sudden changes to routine
  • 👃 Strong scents and perfumes

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Consider your activity level, living space, and time availability. Our Breed Guide categorizes cats by care level and temperament. High-energy breeds like Bengals need more interaction, while calm breeds like Persians are better for quieter homes.

Most cats need brushing 2-3 times per week to prevent matting and reduce shedding. Long-haired breeds like Persians and Maine Coons require daily grooming. Check our Care Tips section for breed-specific grooming recommendations.

Cats are obligate carnivores and need high-quality protein. Choose premium commercial cat food (wet or dry) or consult a vet about homemade diets. Avoid toxic foods like onions, garlic, and chocolate. Read our Nutrition guide in Care Tips for detailed information.

Start by confining them to one room with food, water, and litter box. Gradually introduce them to new areas. Provide hiding spots, toys, and maintain familiar scents. Be patient—adjustment can take weeks. Check our Care Tips for detailed transition guidance.

Kittens and senior cats (7+ years) should visit annually. Adult cats need check-ups every 1-2 years. Regular vet visits help catch health issues early and keep vaccinations current. See our Health section for preventive care tips.

Watch for changes in eating, litter habits, behavior, or appearance. Signs include vomiting, diarrhea, lethargy, excessive grooming, or breathing difficulties. Contact a vet immediately if you notice concerning changes. Read our Health Tips for common symptoms.
